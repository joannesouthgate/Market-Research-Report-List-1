<p><ul><li>Implantable Transcranial Doppler</li><li>Functional Transcranial Doppler (fTCD)</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Transcranial Dopplers (TCD) are devices used to measure blood flow in the brain. There are two types of TCD market: implantable transcranial Doppler and functional transcranial Doppler (fTCD). Implantable TCDs are devices that are surgically placed within the skull to continuously monitor blood flow in real-time. On the other hand, fTCDs are non-invasive devices that measure blood flow changes in specific brain regions during specific tasks, helping researchers study brain function and diagnose certain neurological disorders. Both types of TCDs provide valuable insights into brain health and functioning.</p></p>
