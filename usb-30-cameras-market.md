<p><ul><li>CCD</li><li>CMOS</li></ul></p>
<p><p>USB 3.0 cameras are available in two main market types: CCD and CMOS. CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) cameras use a sensor that converts light into an electrical charge and then measures it. They offer high image quality and are suitable for applications requiring accurate color reproduction. CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) cameras use a different technology that directly converts light into digital signals. They are typically more affordable, consume less power, and provide faster image processing. Both CCD and CMOS cameras are commonly used in various industries and applications, allowing users to capture high-quality images and videos via USB 3.0 connectivity.</p></p>
