<p><p>The market for anti-sensitive toothpaste can be categorized into two types: fluorinated and fluorine-free. Fluorinated toothpaste contains fluoride, which helps to strengthen the tooth enamel and reduce tooth sensitivity. It works by remineralizing the teeth and forming a protective layer. On the other hand, fluorine-free toothpaste does not contain fluoride but instead relies on alternative ingredients such as potassium nitrate or strontium chloride to alleviate tooth sensitivity. These products aim to desensitize the nerve endings in the teeth, providing relief from sensitivity.</p></p>
