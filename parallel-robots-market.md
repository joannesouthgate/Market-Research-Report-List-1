<p><p>Parallel robots, also known as parallel manipulators or parallel kinematic machines, are a type of robotic system that consist of multiple limbs or kinematic chains connected to a common base. These robots are classified based on the number of degrees of freedom (DOF) they possess. Multi-axis parallel robots have more than two kinematic chains, providing enhanced flexibility and precision for complex tasks. In contrast, two-axis parallel robots have two kinematic chains, allowing for simpler and more limited movements. Both types find applications in industries such as manufacturing, assembly, automation, and healthcare due to their speed, accuracy, and dexterity.</p></p>
