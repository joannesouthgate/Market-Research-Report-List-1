<p><p>The wearable sensors market can be classified into two main types: invasive sensors and non-invasive sensors. Invasive sensors are those that require direct contact with the body and monitor physiological signals by penetrating and attaching to the skin. On the other hand, non-invasive wearable sensors don't require any physical intrusion into the body and can collect data from external sources, such as motion, temperature, or heart rate, without any direct contact. Both types of sensors have their own advantages and applications in various industries, including healthcare, sports, and fitness.</p></p>
