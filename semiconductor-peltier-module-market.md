When a current is passed through the module, one side becomes hot and the other side becomes cool. Single-Stage Modules are designed with a single thermoelectric element that enables cooling or heating in applications with relatively small temperature differences. On the other hand, Multi-Stage Modules utilize multiple thermoelectric elements connected in series to achieve more significant temperature differences and provide enhanced cooling or heating capabilities. Both types of modules are used in various industries such as electronics, healthcare, and telecommunications, catering to different temperature control requirements.</p></p>
