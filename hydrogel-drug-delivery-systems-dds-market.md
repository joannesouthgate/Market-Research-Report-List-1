<p><strong>The Hydrogel Drug Delivery Systems (DDS) Market Analysis by types is segmented into:</strong></p>
<p><ul><li>Physical Crosslinked Hydrogels</li><li>Crosslinking Agent Crosslinked Hydrogels</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Hydrogel Drug Delivery Systems (DDS) are a type of drug delivery technology that involves using hydrogels as carriers to transport and release pharmaceutical substances in a controlled manner. There are two main types of hydrogels used in DDS: Physical Crosslinked Hydrogels and Crosslinking Agent Crosslinked Hydrogels. </p><p>Physical Crosslinked Hydrogels are formed by physical interactions between the polymer chains, such as hydrogen bonds. These hydrogels provide a reversible gelation process and can be used to encapsulate and release drugs.</p><p>In contrast, Crosslinking Agent Crosslinked Hydrogels involve the use of a crosslinking agent, which chemically bonds with the polymer chains to form a stable network structure. These hydrogels offer a more controlled release of drugs and improved stability.</p><p>Both types of hydrogels find applications in drug delivery systems, offering various advantages depending on the specific requirements of the pharmaceutical substances being delivered.</p></p>
