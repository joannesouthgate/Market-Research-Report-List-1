As the complexity of chips increases, manual testing becomes inadequate and time-consuming. Test coverage refers to the extent to which a test system can detect faults and defects in chips. <p><p>The Chip Test System market comprises two main types of test equipment: Semiautomatic Test Equipments and Fully Automatic Test Equipments. Semiautomatic Test Equipments involve a combination of manual and automated processes, where operators have to manually load and unload chips for testing, but the testing procedures are automated. On the other hand, Fully Automatic Test Equipments are fully automated, requiring minimal human intervention as they can independently handle chip loading, testing, and unloading processes. Both types play crucial roles in efficiently testing and analyzing chips for quality and performance.</p></p>
