<p><p>The market for regulatory buoys is categorized into three primary applications: offshore, coastal and harbor, and inland waters. Offshore regulatory buoys are deployed in open seas and oceans to mark boundaries, navigation channels, and warn vessels of potential hazards. Coastal and harbor buoys are used to guide ships safely through ports and marinas, and to demarcate restricted zones. Inland water regulatory buoys are installed on rivers, lakes, and reservoirs to aid navigation and notify boaters of speed limits, no-wake zones, and other regulations.</p></p>
