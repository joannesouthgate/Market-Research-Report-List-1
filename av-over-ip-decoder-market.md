<p><p>AV-over-IP (Audio-Visual over Internet Protocol) Decoder refers to a device used to decode audio and video signals transmitted over IP networks. It essentially converts the compressed audio and video data received from an IP network into an uncompressed format that can be easily displayed on a monitor or played through speakers. AV-over-IP Decoders are commonly used in various applications such as video conferencing, digital signage, live events, broadcasting, and control rooms.</p><p>The AV-over-IP Decoder Market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The increasing adoption of IP-based solutions in the audio-visual industry is a major driving factor for market growth. AV-over-IP solutions offer several advantages over traditional analog or point-to-point digital systems, including scalability, flexibility, and cost-effectiveness. With the growing demand for higher resolution and immersive audio-visual experiences, the need for AV-over-IP Decoders is also rising.</p><p>In addition, the rising trend of remote working and virtual meetings has further propelled the demand for AV-over-IP Decoders. The ability to transmit audio and video signals over IP networks enables seamless collaboration and communication, regardless of the physical location of participants. As companies increasingly adopt remote work policies, the AV-over-IP Decoder market is expected to witness substantial growth.</p><p>Moreover, technological advancements in video compression and networking technologies are also driving market growth. <p><p>The AV-over-IP Decoder market consists of three main types: Uncompressed, HD, and Others. HD decoders compress the signals to reduce bandwidth requirements while still delivering excellent quality suitable for high-definition displays. The Others category encompasses different compression technologies and solutions that may offer lower quality or specific functionalities.
