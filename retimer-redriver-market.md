<p><p>Retimers and Redrivers are electronic components used to improve signal integrity in high-speed data transmission systems. Retimers are active devices that receive, regenerate, and retransmit the incoming signals. Redrivers, on the other hand, are passive devices that only reshape and amplify the signals.</p><p>PCIe 4.0, PCIe 5.0, and PCIe 6.0 Retimers are designed specifically for the PCI Express interface, providing signal conditioning and equalization to ensure reliable data transmission at higher speeds.</p><p>Similarly, USB 3.0 and USB 4.0 Retimers enhance the performance of USB interfaces by reconnecting and boosting the signals.</p><p>USB 3.0 Redrivers primarily focus on signal reshaping and amplification for USB 3.0.</p><p>DP 2.0 Retimers specialize in improving signal quality in DisplayPort 2.0 interfaces.</p><p>Lastly, the "Other" market refers to Retimers/Redrivers designed for different interface standards such as SATA, HDMI, and Ethernet, offering signal conditioning and regeneration to ensure accurate data transmission.</p></p>
