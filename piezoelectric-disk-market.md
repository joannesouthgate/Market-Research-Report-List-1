<p><p>A piezoelectric disk, also known as a piezoelectric ceramic disk, is a device that converts mechanical energy into electrical energy and vice versa. <p><ul><li>Unimorph</li><li>Bimorph</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Piezoelectric disk market types refer to different configurations of piezoelectric devices used in various applications. Unimorph disks consist of a single active layer on a passive substrate, enabling simple actuation in one direction. They are used in applications like sensors or simple actuators. Bimorph disks, on the other hand, have two active layers that bend in response to applied voltage, enabling bidirectional actuation. Bimorph disks find applications in loudspeakers, inkjet printers, and ultrasonic motors. Both types offer distinct advantages, depending on the specific application requirements.</p></p>
