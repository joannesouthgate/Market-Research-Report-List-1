<p><p>A dental bridge and crown are dental prosthetic devices used to restore missing teeth or damaged teeth. A bridge, on the other hand, is used to replace one or more missing teeth by bridging the gap between the remaining teeth.</p><p>The dental bridge and crown market has witnessed significant growth in recent years and is expected to continue growing at a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to various factors, including the increasing prevalence of dental disorders such as dental caries and periodontal diseases, rising geriatric population, and a growing awareness about oral health and aesthetics.</p><p>Moreover, advancements in dental technology and materials have also contributed to the market growth. The introduction of ceramic and zirconia materials for crowns and bridges has improved the durability and aesthetics of these dental restorations, thereby increasing their adoption.</p><p>Additionally, the rising demand for cosmetic dentistry and the growing popularity of dental tourism in emerging economies have further boosted the market growth. Dental bridges and crowns are extensively used in cosmetic procedures to enhance the appearance of teeth and improve the overall smile.</p><p>In terms of trends, the market is witnessing a shift towards less invasive and more conservative approaches. The development of minimally invasive techniques, such as adhesive bonding, allows for the preservation of natural tooth structure, which is preferred by both patients and dentists.</p><p>Furthermore, digital dentistry has gained prominence in recent years, enabling the precise design and fabrication of dental restorations. The use of computer-aided design and computer-aided manufacturing (CAD/CAM) systems has facilitated faster and more accurate production of crowns and bridges, reducing the turnaround time for patients.</p><p>Overall, the dental bridge and crown market is expected to continue its growth trajectory, driven by factors such as increasing dental disorders, advancements in dental technology, and a rising emphasis on oral health and aesthetics.</p></p>
