<p><p>Polyisoprene elastomers are classified into different types based on their molecular structure. 1,2-polyisoprene has a linear structure and is commonly used in medical and surgical products. Trans 1,4-polyisoprene has a similar structure to natural rubber and is used in tire manufacturing. Cis-1,4-polyisoprene has a unique polymer chain arrangement, providing high elasticity and resilience, making it suitable for applications requiring flexibility. 3,4-polyisoprene is a rare form that exhibits unique properties and is utilized in niche industries.</p></p>
