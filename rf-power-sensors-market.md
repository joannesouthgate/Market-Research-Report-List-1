<p><p>RF power sensors are used in various industries to measure the power levels of radio frequency signals. Two common types of RF power sensors are heat-based sensors and diode detector-based sensors. Heat-based sensors utilize the principle of thermoelectric effects to convert RF power into heat, which is then measured and converted into electrical signals. On the other hand, diode detector-based sensors use semiconductor diodes to rectify and measure the RF power. Both types offer accurate and reliable measurements of RF power levels in different applications.</p></p>
