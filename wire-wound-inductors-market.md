<p><p>Wire wound inductors are passive electronic components that are used to store energy in the form of magnetic fields. They are made by winding a conductive wire, usually made of copper, around a core material such as ferrite or iron. The wire is wound in a helical or spiral shape to create a coil, and the number of turns in the coil determines the inductance value of the inductor.</p><p>Wire wound inductors are widely used in various applications such as power supplies, automotive electronics, telecommunications, consumer electronics, and industrial electronics. They are used in circuits to filter out unwanted frequencies, control current flow, and provide impedance matching. The increased demand for wireless communication devices, the growing automotive industry, and the rise in renewable energy sources are some of the factors driving the demand for wire wound inductors.</p><p>According to market research, the wire wound inductors market is expected to witness significant growth in the coming years. <p><p>Wire wound inductors are electrical components used to store and release energy in the form of magnetic fields. Shielded wire wound inductors have a shield or casing to protect against electromagnetic interference, ensuring reliable operation in sensitive electronic devices. On the other hand, non-shielded wire wound inductors lack this protective casing, which makes them more cost-effective but potentially more susceptible to interference.
