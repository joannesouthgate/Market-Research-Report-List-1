<p><p>Remote electrocardiogram (ECG) monitoring is a non-invasive procedure used to record the electrical activity of the heart over a period of time. It involves the use of a small device that is attached to the chest and continuously records the heart's electrical signals. The recorded data is then transmitted wirelessly to a healthcare professional who can analyze it for any signs of abnormality or potential cardiac issues.</p><p>The remote ECG monitoring market is experiencing steady growth and is expected to continue expanding at a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of 4.3% during the forecast period. This growth can be attributed to several factors. Firstly, the rising prevalence of cardiovascular diseases, such as arrhythmia and atrial fibrillation, is increasing the demand for remote ECG monitoring as an effective diagnostic tool. The ability to remotely monitor patients' heart activity allows for early detection of potential cardiac issues and prompt intervention.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in technology have led to the development of more compact and user-friendly remote ECG monitoring devices.
