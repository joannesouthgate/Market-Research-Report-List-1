<p><p>Single-phase immersion cooling refers to a method of cooling electronic devices using a liquid medium such as water or oil. Water-based immersion cooling systems use water as the cooling medium, circulating it around the device to absorb heat and dissipate it. Oil-based immersion cooling systems, on the other hand, use oils with high thermal conductivity to transfer heat away from the device. Both systems offer efficient cooling solutions for high-density electronic applications, aiding in reducing energy consumption and ensuring optimal device performance.</p></p>
