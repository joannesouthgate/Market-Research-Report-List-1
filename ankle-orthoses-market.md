<p><p>Ankle orthoses are devices used to support and stabilize the ankle joint. Dynamic orthotics are designed to provide flexibility and allow controlled movement while offering support and protection to the ankle. They use springs or hinges to help with foot movement and promote healing. On the other hand, static orthotics are rigid devices that limit motion and provide more stability to the ankle. They are often used to immobilize the joint and aid in the recovery of injuries or reduce pain. Both types of orthotics are used in the ankle orthoses market to cater to different patient needs.</p></p>
