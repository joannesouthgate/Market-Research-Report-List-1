Polymer PTCs are made from conductive polymers that exhibit a positive temperature coefficient of resistance, leading to their use in applications such as overcurrent protection. Ceramic PTCs, on the other hand, are made from a ceramic material with a positive temperature coefficient, which makes them suitable for applications like motor starting and heating elements. Both polymer and ceramic PTC resistors provide efficient temperature-dependent resistance control.</p></p>
