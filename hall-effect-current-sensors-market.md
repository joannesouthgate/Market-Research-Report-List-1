<p><p>Hall Effect current sensors are used to measure current in electrical systems. Open-loop Hall Effect current sensors have a direct connection between the source and the load and provide an output voltage proportional to the current. Closed-loop Hall Effect current sensors, on the other hand, utilize a feedback mechanism to adjust the output signal, making them more accurate and linear. They provide a digital output that is proportional to the current being measured. Both types have their advantages and are widely used in applications such as automotive, industrial, and power management systems.</p></p>
