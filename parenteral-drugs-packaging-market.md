<p><p>Parenteral drugs packaging market types refer to different materials used for packaging injectable drugs. Polyvinyl Chloride (PVC) and Polyolefin are two common types. PVC is a widely used material due to its flexibility and transparency. It provides good barrier properties and compatibility with drugs. On the other hand, Polyolefin is a newer, more advanced material that offers enhanced drug compatibility, chemical resistance, and better cold-forming properties. Both materials are extensively used in the parenteral drugs packaging market for ensuring the safety, stability, and quality of injectable medicines.</p></p>

<p><p>The parenteral drugs packaging market primarily caters to two categories of drugs: large volume parenteral (LVP) and small volume parenteral (SVP). LVP refers to drugs packaged in larger quantities (usually more than 100 mL), which are administered intravenously and require controlled infusion rates. On the other hand, SVP indicates drugs packaged in smaller volumes (typically less than 100 mL), which are often administered via syringes or infusion pumps. Both LVP and SVP play crucial roles in various medical applications, providing patients with essential medications in a safe and efficient manner.</p></p>
