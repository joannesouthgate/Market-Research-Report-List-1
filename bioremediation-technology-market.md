<p><p>Bioremediation technology refers to methods used to clean up contaminated environments using living organisms. Bioreactors are devices that provide a controlled environment for microorganisms to degrade pollutants. Biostimulation involves stimulating the growth and activity of natural occurring microorganisms to enhance their ability to degrade contaminants. Bioaugmentation involves introducing specific microorganisms to enhance the degradation process. Fungal remediation uses fungi to break down contaminants. Phytoremediation uses plants to remove, degrade, or contain pollutants. Land-based treatments involve excavating contaminated soil and treating it through various methods to remove or neutralize contaminants.</p></p>
