<p><p>Evolved Gas Analysis (EGA) is a technique used to identify and analyze gases released during a controlled sample heating process. There are four main types of EGA market: FTIR (Fourier Transform Infrared Spectroscopy), QMS (Quadrupole Mass Spectrometry), GCMS (Gas Chromatography-Mass Spectrometry), and ELIF (Evolved Laser Ionization Fourier Transform Mass Spectrometry). FTIR measures the gases' absorption of infrared light, QMS identifies and quantifies ions according to their mass-to-charge ratio, GCMS separates and analyzes gas components based on their chemical properties, and ELIF uses laser ionization to measure the molecular mass of the evolved gases.</p></p>
