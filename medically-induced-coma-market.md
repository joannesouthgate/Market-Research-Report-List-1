<p><p>Medically induced coma refers to a state in which a patient is placed under sedation to induce a temporary loss of consciousness. Propofol and barbiturates are common drugs used to induce medically induced coma. Propofol is a short-acting anesthetic agent that provides rapid induction and recovery, often used in critical care settings. Barbiturates, such as thiopental and pentobarbital, have a longer duration of action and are commonly used to manage increased intracranial pressure in patients with traumatic brain injuries. Both propofol and barbiturates are administered intravenously and closely monitored by medical professionals.</p></p>
