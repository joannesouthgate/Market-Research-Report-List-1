<p><p>The smoked mackerel market can be categorized into two main types: hot-smoke and cold-smoke. Hot-smoke refers to a method where the mackerel is cooked and smoked at the same time, resulting in a moist and flaky texture with a stronger smoky flavor. On the other hand, cold-smoke involves smoking the mackerel at a lower temperature over a longer period, giving it a milder, delicate smoky taste and a firmer texture. Both methods offer distinct flavor profiles, allowing consumers to choose based on their personal preferences.</p></p>
