<p><p>Resorbable collagen membranes are biocompatible materials commonly used in guided tissue regeneration (GTR) procedures. These membranes are made from purified collagen extracted from animal sources, such as bovine or porcine, and are designed to facilitate the regeneration of soft and hard tissues damaged by periodontal disease or dental implants.</p><p>The resorbable collagen membrane market is experiencing significant growth due to several factors. Firstly, the increasing prevalence of dental disorders and periodontal diseases, such as gingivitis and periodontitis, is driving the demand for resorbable collagen membranes as they aid in the regeneration of damaged tissues. Additionally, the rise in the number of dental implant procedures being performed worldwide is boosting market growth.</p><p>Furthermore, advancements in material technology and the development of new manufacturing techniques have improved the performance of resorbable collagen membranes, leading to enhanced clinical outcomes. These advancements include the incorporation of growth factors or drugs into the membrane, which can promote faster and more effective tissue regeneration.</p><p>In terms of market trends, there is a growing preference for resorbable collagen membranes over non-resorbable membranes due to their ability to naturally degrade in the body, eliminating the need for a secondary surgery for removal. The demand for resorbable collagen membranes with improved handling properties and biocompatibility is also on the rise.</p><p>Overall, the resorbable collagen membrane market is expected to witness significant growth during the forecast period, with a projected CAGR of 11.40%. This growth can be attributed to the increasing prevalence of dental disorders, the rise in dental implant procedures, advancements in material technology, and the preference for resorbable membranes.
