<p><p>Geographic Information Systems (GIS) is a technology that captures, stores, analyzes, and manages geographical data. The market types in GIS include numeric data, vector data, raster data, and others. Numeric data represents information that can be measured, such as population density or temperature. Vector data uses points, lines, and polygons to represent geographic features, such as roads or boundary lines. Raster data comprises grids of cells, representing attributes like satellite images or elevation models. The "others" category includes non-traditional data formats, such as 3D models or topological data, which provide additional layers of information in GIS analysis.</p></p>
