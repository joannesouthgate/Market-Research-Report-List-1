<p><p>Personal 3D printers come in various market types. Poly-jet printers use liquid photopolymers, creating precise and multi-colored objects. Fuse Deposition Modeling (FDM) printers work by extruding plastic filaments layer by layer. Selective Laser Sintering (SLS) printers utilize lasers to fuse powdered materials like metal or nylon. Stereo Lithography printers employ UV lasers to solidify liquid resin, providing high-resolution prints. Lastly, there are other types of personal 3D printers available that use different techniques like digital light processing or binder jetting to create objects from various materials.</p></p>
