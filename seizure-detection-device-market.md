<p><p>The seizure detection device market consists of three types: conventional, wearable, and implantable devices. Conventional seizure detection devices are non-invasive and typically used in healthcare facilities for monitoring and diagnosing seizures. Wearable seizure detection devices are portable and can be worn by individuals to track and alert them about potential seizures. Implantable seizure detection devices are surgically implanted into the body and continuously monitor brain activity, providing real-time seizure detection and potential intervention options. These devices aim to improve the diagnosis and management of seizures for better patient outcomes.</p></p>
