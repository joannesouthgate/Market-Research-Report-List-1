<p><ul><li>Biological Monitoring</li><li>Chemical Monitoring</li><li>Mechanical Monitoring</li></ul></p>
<p><p>Sterilization monitoring is essential in various industries to ensure the effectiveness of sterilization processes. Biological monitoring involves using living organisms to validate the efficiency of sterilization, such as bacteria spores. Chemical monitoring employs indicators that change color or form upon exposure to the sterilization process. Mechanical monitoring employs sensors or indicators to monitor the physical parameters of sterilization equipment, such as temperature, pressure, or time. Each type of monitoring serves as a quality control measure, providing assurance that the sterilization process is successfully eliminating harmful microorganisms.</p></p>
