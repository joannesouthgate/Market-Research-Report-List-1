<p><p>Esophageal video capsule endoscopy is a medical imaging technology used for diagnosing and monitoring diseases in the esophagus. It consists of a small capsule equipped with a camera chip to capture images inside the esophagus. There are two types of camera chips used in this market: CMOS (Complementary Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor) photosensitive chip and CCD (Charge-Coupled Device) photosensitive chip. CMOS chips are generally cheaper, consume less power, and provide better image quality in low light conditions. On the other hand, CCD chips offer higher image resolution and are more suitable for detailed image capture in brighter environments.</p></p>
