<p><p>A magnetic coupling transformer is a device that utilizes magnetic coupling to transfer electrical energy from one circuit to another. It consists of two coils, a primary and a secondary, which are magnetically coupled through a shared magnetic core. This design allows for galvanic isolation between the primary and secondary circuits, enabling the safe transfer of power without the need for a direct electrical connection.</p><p>The magnetic coupling transformer market is expected to experience significant growth in the coming years. The increasing demand for energy-efficient power transmission systems, coupled with the rising need for galvanic isolation in various applications, is driving the market growth. <p><p>Magnetic coupling transformers are available in various market types based on their impedance ratios. The 1:1 impedance ratio transformers are designed to provide equal voltage transfer between the primary and secondary windings. The 4:1 impedance ratio transformers, on the other hand, offer a fourfold increase in voltage output compared to the input. Similarly, the 8:1 impedance ratio transformers provide eight times higher voltage output. Apart from these, there are other market types that offer different impedance ratios fulfilling specific requirements. These impedance ratios help cater to different power distribution needs in various applications.</p></p>
