<p><p>Implantable Ventricular Assist Devices (VADs) are mechanical devices used to support the weakened heart by helping it pump blood. There are three main types of VADs: Left Ventricular Assist Devices (LVADs) are implanted on the left side of the heart to support its pumping, Right Ventricular Assist Devices (RVADs) are placed on the right side, and Bi-ventricular Assist Devices (BIVADs) involve both sides of the heart. Each VAD type has its own unique features and benefits, offering crucial support to patients with severe heart failure or waiting for a heart transplant.</p></p>
