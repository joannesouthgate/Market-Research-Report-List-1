<p><p>Partial hip replacement is a surgical procedure commonly used to treat hip joint conditions. The Cemented Hip Replacement market involves using bone cement to attach the prosthetic components to the natural bone. Cementless Hip Replacement, on the other hand, relies on the bone's natural ability to grow into the prosthesis without the need for cement. Hybrid Hip Replacement combines both cemented and cementless techniques, providing stability and fixation. These market types offer alternative solutions for patients with differing bone qualities and surgical preferences.</p></p>
