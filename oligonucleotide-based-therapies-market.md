<p><p>Oligonucleotide-based therapies refer to a type of treatment that utilizes short sequences of nucleic acids (oligonucleotides) to target specific disease-causing genes or proteins. There are different types of oligonucleotide-based therapies available in the market. Antisense oligonucleotides bind to the target RNA, preventing the synthesis of a harmful protein. Aptamers are single-stranded DNA or RNA molecules that bind to specific target molecules, such as proteins, inhibiting their activity. Other types of oligonucleotide-based therapies include ribozymes and small interfering RNAs (siRNAs), which regulate gene expression. These therapies hold immense potential for treating various diseases at the genetic level.</p></p>
